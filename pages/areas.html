Areas & Surface Areas

Formulas, diagrams and calculators for flat shapes and 3D solids. Click any card to open its calculator.

What is Area?

Area is the measure of two-dimensional space enclosed within a boundary, expressed in square units (cm², m², km²). It answers the question: how much surface does this shape cover?

For solid objects we calculate surface area — the total area of every outer face. Understanding area is fundamental to architecture, engineering, design, and data visualisation.

Shape Calculators

Circle

Every point on the boundary is equidistant from the centre.
A = π r²
+
The area grows with the square of the radius — doubling r quadruples the area. π is irrational; its digits never repeat.

Rectangle

Four right angles; opposite sides parallel and equal.
A = l × b
+
A square is a special rectangle where l = b, giving A = l². Perimeter = 2(l + b). All rectangles can tile a plane without gaps.

Triangle

Simplest polygon — three sides, three angles.
A = ½ × b × h
+
h must be perpendicular to the base. If only three sides are known use Heron's formula: A = √(s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)), s = (a+b+c)/2.

Parallelogram

Both pairs of opposite sides are parallel and equal.
A = b × h
+
A parallelogram can be sheared into a rectangle of equal area — hence A = b × h. The height is always perpendicular to the base, not the slant side.

Trapezium

Exactly one pair of parallel sides (a and b).
A = ½ (a+b) × h
+
Think of it as the average of two rectangles: one of base a, one of base b, both of height h. Common in architecture and cross-section engineering problems.

Square

All four sides equal; all four angles 90°.
A = a²
+
The word square in algebra (x²) comes directly from this shape — multiplying a number by itself gives the area of a square with that side length. Diagonal = a√2.

Ellipse

Oval with two focal points. A circle is an ellipse where a = b.
A = π × a × b
+
Planets orbit the Sun in ellipses (Kepler's First Law, 1609). The formula πab directly generalises πr². Interestingly the perimeter of an ellipse has no simple closed form.

Sphere — Surface Area

Perfectly round 3D solid. Every point equidistant from the centre.
SA = 4π r²
+
The surface of a sphere equals exactly 4 great circles of radius r — proved by Archimedes. The surface-area-to-volume ratio is 3/r, which limits biological cell size.
