Areas & Surface Areas

Learn to measure 2D regions and 3D surfaces through diagrams, formulas, and real-world context. Click any card to open its calculator.

What is Area?

Area measures the two-dimensional space enclosed within a boundary — expressed in square units (cm², m², km²). It answers: "How much surface does this shape cover?"

For 3D objects we compute surface area — the total area of all outer faces. Understanding area underpins architecture, land surveying, material estimation, and computer graphics.

Shape Calculators

Circle

Perfectly round plane figure — every point equidistant from centre.
A = π r²
+
The circle's area grows with the square of the radius. Doubling r quadruples A. π (≈ 3.14159) is irrational — its digits never repeat and never end.

Rectangle

Quadrilateral with four right angles and opposite sides equal.
A = l × b
+
A rectangle's area = product of its two side lengths. A square is a special rectangle (l = b), so A = l². Perimeter = 2(l + b).

Triangle

Three-sided polygon — half the area of the parallelogram it forms.
A = ½ × b × h
+
If only three sides are known, use Heron's formula: A = √(s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)), where s = (a+b+c)/2. Height h must be perpendicular to the base.

Parallelogram

Quadrilateral with both pairs of opposite sides parallel and equal.
A = b × h
+
A parallelogram can be sheared into a rectangle with same base and height — hence same area. h is always perpendicular to the base, not the slanted side.

Trapezium

Quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides (a and b).
A = ½(a + b) × h
+
Think of a trapezium as the average of two rectangles — one with base a and one with base b — giving (a+b)/2 × h. Common in architecture and cross-section calculations.

Square

Regular quadrilateral — all four sides equal and all angles 90°.
A = a²
+
The word "square" in algebra (x²) comes from this shape. Multiplying a number by itself gives the area of a square with that side. Its diagonal = a√2.

Ellipse

Oval curve with two focal points. A circle is a special ellipse (a = b).
A = π × a × b
+
Planets orbit the Sun in ellipses (Kepler's First Law). The formula πab generalises πr² — replace r² with a×b. The perimeter of an ellipse has no simple exact formula.

Sphere (Surface Area)

Perfectly round 3D solid — every surface point equidistant from centre.
SA = 4π r²
+
The surface area of a sphere equals exactly 4 circles of radius r — a beautiful result proved by Archimedes. Surface-area-to-volume ratio = 3/r, key in biology (cell size limits).
