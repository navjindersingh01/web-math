Mathematical Series

A series is the sum of a sequence. Learn about finite sums, convergence and divergence through interactive calculators with full working.

Series vs. Sequence

A sequence is an ordered list: 1, 2, 3, 4… A series is what you get when you add them up: 1+2+3+4+… = Sₙ.

Series can be finite (a fixed number of terms) or infinite. An infinite series either converges to a finite value or diverges to ±∞. Convergence is one of the most profound ideas in all of mathematics and underpins calculus, physics and engineering.

General form:Sₙ = a₁ + a₂ + … + aₙ = Σ aᵢ, i = 1 to n

Series Calculators

Arithmetic Series

Sum of n terms with first term a and common difference d.
Sₙ = n/2 · (2a + (n−1)d)
+
Young Gauss summed 1 to 100 = 5050 instantly by pairing terms: (1+100)+(2+99)+… = 50 pairs of 101. The formula encodes this: Sₙ = n × (average of first and last term).

Geometric Series

Sum of n terms with first term a and common ratio r (r ≠ 1).
Sₙ = a(1 − rⁿ) / (1 − r)
+
Compound interest over n periods is a geometric series. Binary storage is too: 1, 2, 4, 8… bytes (a=1, r=2). The formula is derived by multiplying the series by r, then subtracting.

Infinite Geometric Series

Sum of infinitely many terms — converges only when |r| < 1.
S∞ = a / (1 − r)
+
Zeno's Paradox: to cross a room you first cross half, then half again… yet you arrive. The sum ½ + ¼ + ⅛ + … = 1 resolves this. Diverges for |r| ≥ 1.

Harmonic Series

1 + ½ + ⅓ + ¼ + … — the most famous divergent series.
Hₙ = Σ 1/k, k = 1 to n
+
Despite each term shrinking to zero, the sum grows without bound — proved by Nicole Oresme (14th century). Growth is slow: Hₙ ≈ ln(n) + γ where γ ≈ 0.5772 is the Euler–Mascheroni constant.
